Number System

Divisibility, LCM/HCF, remainders, surds, indices — the foundation of all quant

TCS TQT has 3–5 number system questions per exam. Divisibility rules and remainders are the most tested subtopics.
⚡ Divisibility Rules (Must memorise)
  • Div by 2: Last digit is 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8
  • Div by 3: Sum of all digits is divisible by 3. (e.g. 123 → 1+2+3=6 ✓)
  • Div by 4: Last two digits form a number divisible by 4. (e.g. 1732 → 32÷4=8 ✓)
  • Div by 6: Divisible by both 2 AND 3
  • Div by 7: Double the last digit, subtract from remaining number; repeat until small. (e.g. 343 → 34−6=28, 28÷7=4 ✓)
  • Div by 8: Last three digits divisible by 8. (e.g. 1000 → 000÷8=0 ✓)
  • Div by 9: Sum of all digits divisible by 9. (e.g. 729 → 7+2+9=18 ✓)
  • Div by 11: (Sum of digits at odd positions) − (Sum at even positions) = 0 or divisible by 11. (e.g. 1331 → (1+3)−(3+1)=0 ✓)
  • Div by 25: Last two digits are 00, 25, 50, or 75
🔢 HCF & LCM — Formulas & Methods
Key relationship HCF × LCM = Product of the two numbers
HCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b) = a × b

For fractions HCF of fractions = HCF of numerators / LCM of denominators
LCM of fractions = LCM of numerators / HCF of denominators
  • Division method for HCF: Divide larger by smaller, take remainder, repeat. Last non-zero remainder = HCF.
  • LCM by prime factorisation: Write each number as product of primes. LCM = product of highest power of each prime present.
  • HCF of numbers that leave same remainder r when divided by them: HCF = HCF of their differences.
Example
Find HCF of 36 and 48 → 48 = 36×1 + 12; 36 = 12×3 + 0 → HCF = 12
LCM = (36 × 48) / 12 = 144
➗ Remainders — Patterns & Tricks
  • Cyclicity of remainders: Find the repeating cycle of rⁿ mod m, then use (n mod cycle_length) to find the remainder quickly.
  • Remainder of aⁿ / m: Find a mod m, then raise to power n, taking mod at each step.
  • Fermat's Little Theorem: If p is prime and gcd(a,p)=1, then a^(p-1) ≡ 1 (mod p). Very useful for TCS remainder questions.
  • Sum of remainders: Remainder of (a+b)/m = [remainder(a/m) + remainder(b/m)] mod m
  • Product of remainders: Remainder of (a×b)/m = [remainder(a/m) × remainder(b/m)] mod m
Classic TCS pattern: Find remainder of 7⁵⁰ ÷ 6
7 mod 6 = 1 → 7ⁿ mod 6 = 1ⁿ = 1 for any n → Remainder = 1
Find last two digits of 7⁴ = 2401 → last two digits = 01
Pattern of 7ⁿ last 2 digits: 07, 49, 43, 01, 07, 49… (cycle 4) → 7¹⁰⁰ → 100/4=25 → cycle position 4 → 01
📊 Number Types & Properties
  • Perfect squares up to 30²: 1,4,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100,121,144,169,196,225,256,289,324,361,400,441,484,529,576,625,676,729,784,841,900
  • Perfect cubes: 1,8,27,64,125,216,343,512,729,1000
  • Sum of first n natural numbers: n(n+1)/2
  • Sum of first n odd numbers: n²
  • Sum of squares of first n: n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
  • Sum of cubes of first n: [n(n+1)/2]²
  • Factorial: n! = n×(n−1)×…×1
    0! = 1, 1! = 1
  • Trailing zeros in n!: Count factors of 5: ⌊n/5⌋ + ⌊n/25⌋ + ⌊n/125⌋ + …
🧮 Surds & Indices
aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ | aᵐ / aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ | (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ
a⁰ = 1 | a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ | aᵐ/ⁿ = ⁿ√(aᵐ)
√(ab) = √a × √b | √(a/b) = √a/√b
Rationalising: multiply numerator and denominator by conjugate
1/(√a+√b) × (√a−√b)/(√a−√b) = (√a−√b)/(a−b)

Arithmetic — Core Topics

Percentages, Profit & Loss, Time & Work, Speed & Distance, Simple & Compound Interest

Arithmetic accounts for 40–50% of TCS TQT quant. Master percentages and time-work first — they appear in almost every exam.
📊 Percentages — Formulas & Shortcuts
% change = (Change / Original) × 100
A is what % of B = (A/B) × 100
A% of B = B% of A ← massive time-saver!

Successive % changes If x% increase then y% decrease: Net = x − y − (xy/100)
Two successive increases of a%: Net = 2a + a²/100
  • Percentage fraction equivalents (memorise these for speed):
    1/8=12.5%, 1/6=16.67%, 1/5=20%, 1/4=25%, 1/3=33.33%, 3/8=37.5%, 2/5=40%, 1/2=50%, 3/5=60%, 5/8=62.5%, 2/3=66.67%, 3/4=75%, 4/5=80%, 5/6=83.33%, 7/8=87.5%
  • If A is x% more than B, then B is less than A by: x/(100+x) × 100 %
  • If A is x% less than B, then B is more than A by: x/(100−x) × 100 %
Speed trick — A is 25% more than B. B is less than A by?
25/(100+25) × 100 = 25/125 × 100 = 20%
💰 Profit & Loss
Profit = SP − CP | Loss = CP − SP
Profit % = (Profit/CP) × 100
SP = CP × (100 + P%)/100 | CP = SP × 100/(100 + P%)

Discount Discount % = (Discount/Marked Price) × 100
SP = MP × (100 − D%)/100

Successive discounts d1%, d2% Net effective discount = d1 + d2 − d1×d2/100
  • If cost price of x items = selling price of y items: Profit% = (x−y)/y × 100
  • Dishonest merchant using false weight: Profit% = (True weight − False weight)/False weight × 100
  • Overall profit/loss when two items sold at same price, one at x% profit one at x% loss: Always a LOSS of (x²/100)%
Classic TCS pattern
Two articles sold for ₹1200 each. One at 20% profit, one at 20% loss.
Net loss = 20²/100 = 4% (always loss when same SP, same % profit and loss)
🏦 Simple & Compound Interest
Simple Interest SI = P × R × T / 100
A = P + SI = P(1 + RT/100)

Compound Interest A = P × (1 + R/100)ⁿ
CI = A − P = P[(1 + R/100)ⁿ − 1]

Half-yearly compounding A = P × (1 + R/200)²ⁿ

CI vs SI difference (2 years) CI − SI = P(R/100)²
  • Rule of 72: Years to double at r% CI ≈ 72/r. At 12% → doubles in 6 years.
  • Effective annual rate for half-yearly: (1 + r/200)² − 1
  • Population problems: Use CI formula with growth/decay rate.
SI vs CI difference — TCS pattern
P=₹10,000, R=10%, T=2 years
CI−SI = P(R/100)² = 10000 × (0.1)² = ₹100
⚙️ Time & Work
If A does work in 'a' days → A's 1-day work = 1/a
Combined rate = 1/a + 1/b + 1/c …
Time together = 1 / (combined rate)

A and B together formula Time = ab/(a+b)
  • A can do work in a days, B in b days, C in c days. A+B work for x days, then C alone finishes:
    Remaining work = 1 − x(1/a + 1/b) → Time for C = remaining / (1/c)
  • Pipes & Cisterns: Inlet pipe fills in a hours (rate +1/a). Outlet pipe empties in b hours (rate −1/b). Net rate = 1/a − 1/b.
  • Men × Days = Constant work. If M₁D₁ = M₂D₂ (same work), use: M₁D₁H₁/W₁ = M₂D₂H₂/W₂
  • Efficiency method: Assume total work = LCM of all individual times. Assign daily work units. Much faster than fraction method!
Efficiency method — A finishes in 12 days, B in 15 days
Total work = LCM(12,15) = 60 units
A's rate = 60/12 = 5 units/day; B's rate = 60/15 = 4 units/day
Together = 9 units/day → Time = 60/9 = 6⅔ days
🚂 Speed, Distance & Time
Distance = Speed × Time
Average Speed (two equal distances) = 2uv/(u+v) ← NOT (u+v)/2 !

Trains Same direction: Relative speed = S1 − S2
Opposite direction: Relative speed = S1 + S2
Time to cross = (L1 + L2) / Relative Speed

Boats & Streams Downstream speed = B + R | Upstream speed = B − R
Boat speed B = (D+U)/2 | Stream speed R = (D−U)/2
  • Unit conversions: km/h to m/s → multiply by 5/18. m/s to km/h → multiply by 18/5.
  • If speed increases by a/b of original, time to cover same distance decreases by a/(a+b).
  • Time to cross a pole/person = Train length / Train speed
  • Time to cross a bridge = (Train length + Bridge length) / Train speed
Average speed trap — TCS pattern
A travels 60 km/h onward and 40 km/h return. Average speed?
= 2×60×40/(60+40) = 4800/100 = 48 km/h (NOT 50!)
⚖️ Ratio, Proportion & Mixtures
a:b = c:d → a×d = b×c (cross multiply)
Compounded ratio of a:b and c:d = ac:bd

Alligation / Mixture rule (Cheaper qty)/(Costlier qty) = (Costlier − Mean) / (Mean − Cheaper)

Mixture replacement After n replacements: Final fraction of original = (1 − x/V)ⁿ
where x = quantity removed each time, V = total volume
  • If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, then A:B:C = 8:12:15 (multiply B to match)
  • Partnership: Profit split is proportional to (Capital × Time).
  • Milk-water ratio problems: Use alligation. Draw a cross, put mean price in centre, individual prices on sides. Diagonal difference gives ratio.
Alligation — mix milk costing ₹15/L and ₹20/L to get ₹17/L
Cheaper side: 20−17=3 | Costlier side: 17−15=2
Ratio of cheaper:costlier = 3:2

Algebra

Linear & quadratic equations, progressions (AP/GP), logarithms, functions

TCS TQT tests AP/GP series and quadratic equations heavily. Know all AP/GP formulas cold — they appear in 3–4 questions each exam.
➕ Linear & Quadratic Equations
Quadratic: ax² + bx + c = 0 x = [−b ± √(b²−4ac)] / 2a
Sum of roots = −b/a | Product of roots = c/a
Discriminant D = b²−4ac
D>0: two real distinct roots | D=0: equal roots | D<0: no real roots
  • Sum & product shortcut: If roots are α and β, equation is x² − (α+β)x + αβ = 0
  • System of linear equations: Unique solution when |a1/a2 ≠ b1/b2|. No solution when a1/a2 = b1/b2 ≠ c1/c2. Infinite when all ratios equal.
  • Number problems: Let digits be t (tens) and u (units). Number = 10t + u. Reversed = 10u + t.
📈 Arithmetic Progressions (AP)
AP: a, a+d, a+2d, … (common difference d) nᵗʰ term: Tₙ = a + (n−1)d
Sum of n terms: Sₙ = n/2 × [2a + (n−1)d]
Sₙ = n/2 × (first term + last term)

If Tₙ is given, n = (Tₙ − a)/d + 1
Middle term of AP = (first + last)/2
Find sum of AP: 5, 9, 13, … up to 20 terms
a=5, d=4, n=20
S₂₀ = 20/2 × [2×5 + 19×4] = 10 × [10+76] = 10 × 86 = 860
📉 Geometric Progressions (GP)
GP: a, ar, ar², … (common ratio r) nᵗʰ term: Tₙ = arⁿ⁻¹
Sum of n terms: Sₙ = a(rⁿ−1)/(r−1) for r≠1
Sum of infinite GP (|r|<1): S∞ = a/(1−r)

Geometric mean of a and b = √(ab)
If three numbers in GP: b² = ac
Find sum of GP: 2, 6, 18, 54 … (6 terms)
a=2, r=3, n=6
S₆ = 2(3⁶−1)/(3−1) = 2×728/2 = 728
🔢 Logarithms
log_a(mn) = log_a(m) + log_a(n)
log_a(m/n) = log_a(m) − log_a(n)
log_a(mⁿ) = n × log_a(m)
log_a(a) = 1 | log_a(1) = 0 | log_a(b) = 1/log_b(a)
Change of base: log_a(b) = log(b)/log(a)
log₁₀(2) ≈ 0.301 | log₁₀(3) ≈ 0.477
🔡 Important Algebraic Identities
(a+b)² = a²+2ab+b²
(a−b)² = a²−2ab+b²
a²−b² = (a+b)(a−b)
(a+b)³ = a³+3a²b+3ab²+b³
(a−b)³ = a³−3a²b+3ab²−b³
a³+b³ = (a+b)(a²−ab+b²)
a³−b³ = (a−b)(a²+ab+b²)
(a+b+c)² = a²+b²+c²+2ab+2bc+2ca

Geometry & Mensuration

Lines, triangles, circles, polygons, 2D and 3D shapes — areas, volumes, and properties

Mensuration (area/volume) questions appear in every TCS TQT exam. Learn formulas for all standard shapes. Triangle properties are heavily tested.
📐 Triangle Properties
  • Angle sum = 180°. Exterior angle = sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.
  • Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c² (right triangle, c = hypotenuse)
  • Common Pythagorean triples: 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, 7-24-25, 9-40-41. Multiples also work (6-8-10, etc.)
  • Area formulas: (1/2)×base×height | (1/2)×a×b×sinC | √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)] (Heron's, s = semi-perimeter)
  • Equilateral triangle: Area = (√3/4)a² | Height = (√3/2)a
  • Median divides triangle into 2 equal areas. Centroid divides median in 2:1.
  • Similar triangles: Ratio of areas = (ratio of sides)²
⭕ Circles
Area = πr² | Circumference = 2πr
Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr (θ in degrees)
Sector area = (θ/360) × πr²
Segment area = Sector area − Triangle area
  • Angle in a semicircle = 90° (angle in a semicircle is always a right angle)
  • Tangent ⊥ radius at the point of contact.
  • Two tangents from an external point are equal in length.
  • Tangent-Chord angle = inscribed angle in alternate segment.
  • Chord distance from centre: If chord length = 2l, distance from centre = d, then r² = l² + d²
🔷 Quadrilaterals & Polygons
Rectangle: Area = l×b | Diagonal = √(l²+b²) | Perimeter = 2(l+b)
Square: Area = a² | Diagonal = a√2 | Perimeter = 4a
Parallelogram: Area = base × height = ab×sinθ
Rhombus: Area = (d1×d2)/2 (diagonals)
Trapezium: Area = (1/2)×(sum of parallel sides)×height

Sum of interior angles of n-gon = (n−2)×180°
Each interior angle of regular n-gon = (n−2)×180°/n
📦 3D Mensuration

  • If a sphere is melted into cylinders: Volume of sphere = n × Volume of each cylinder
  • Diagonal of cuboid: √(l² + b² + h²)
  • When cube is cut into n equal smaller cubes: Side of smaller = a/n^(1/3)
📏 Coordinate Geometry
Distance = √[(x2−x1)² + (y2−y1)²]
Midpoint = ((x1+x2)/2, (y1+y2)/2)
Slope m = (y2−y1)/(x2−x1)
Line: y = mx + c (c = y-intercept)
Parallel lines: m1 = m2 | Perpendicular: m1×m2 = −1
Distance from point (x₀,y₀) to line ax+by+c=0: |ax₀+by₀+c|/√(a²+b²)

Permutations, Combinations & Probability

Counting, arrangements, selections, and probability calculations

P&C and Probability together form 5–8 questions in TCS TQT. Understand when to use P vs C before jumping to formulas.
🔀 Fundamental Counting Principle
  • Multiplication rule: If event A can occur in m ways AND then event B in n ways → Total = m × n ways.
  • Addition rule: If event A can occur in m ways OR event B in n ways (mutually exclusive) → Total = m + n ways.
  • Key decision: P or C?
    — ORDER MATTERS → Permutation (arrangement)
    — ORDER DOESN'T MATTER → Combination (selection)
🔢 Permutations
ⁿPᵣ = n! / (n−r)! — arrange r items from n
Arrangements of n items = n!
Arrangements with repetitions: n!/p!q!r!… (p,q,r = freq of repeated items)

Circular arrangements (n−1)! — circular arrangement of n items
(n−1)!/2 — necklace/keychain (can flip)

Items always TOGETHER: treat as 1 block → (n−k+1)! × k! (k = no. of items in block)
How many ways to arrange letters of MISSISSIPPI?
Total letters=11, M=1, I=4, S=4, P=2
= 11! / (1!×4!×4!×2!) = 34,650
🎯 Combinations
ⁿCᵣ = n! / [r!(n−r)!]
ⁿCᵣ = ⁿCₙ₋ᵣ ← very useful shortcut
ⁿC₀ = ⁿCₙ = 1 | ⁿC₁ = n

Important identities ⁿCᵣ + ⁿCᵣ₋₁ = ⁿ⁺¹Cᵣ
Sum of all combinations = 2ⁿ (all subsets of n items)
  • Selecting from groups: Select p from group A (size m) AND q from group B (size n): ᵐCₚ × ⁿCᵍ
  • At least one / at most one: Use complement. P(at least 1) = 1 − P(none)
  • Handshakes / diagonals: n people shaking hands = ⁿC₂. Diagonals in n-gon = ⁿC₂ − n
Committee of 3 men, 2 women from 5 men, 4 women
⁵C₃ × ⁴C₂ = 10 × 6 = 60 ways
🎲 Probability
P(Event) = Favourable outcomes / Total outcomes
P(A') = 1 − P(A) (complement)
P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B)
P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B) — only if A, B INDEPENDENT
P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B) — conditional probability
  • Mutually exclusive events: P(A ∩ B) = 0 → P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)
  • Cards: 52 cards total. 4 suits × 13 cards each. 4 aces, 4 kings, 4 queens, 4 jacks. 26 red, 26 black.
  • Dice: Two dice → 36 total outcomes. P(sum=7) = 6/36 = 1/6 (highest). P(sum=2 or 12) = 1/36 (lowest).
  • Balls in bag (without replacement): P(2nd) depends on what was drawn 1st.
P(at least one head) in 3 coin tosses
= 1 − P(no heads) = 1 − (1/2)³ = 1 − 1/8 = 7/8

Data Interpretation & Miscellaneous

Bar charts, tables, pie charts, age problems, calendar, clocks

DI typically appears as 1–2 sets (4–6 questions) in TCS TQT. Speed is critical — practice reading charts fast. Age & clock problems are very common.
📊 Data Interpretation — Strategy
  • Read the title and labels first. Know what unit is being used (thousands, lakhs, %). Missing this = guaranteed wrong answers.
  • For bar charts: Estimate visually first, then calculate. Approximate before doing full computation.
  • For pie charts: Central angle for x% = x/100 × 360°. % share = (value/total) × 100.
  • Percentage change between years: (New − Old)/Old × 100.
  • Ratio questions in DI: Reduce to simplest form before comparing.
  • Two-step questions: Read carefully — "what % of total" vs "what % increase" are different questions.
👴 Age Problems
Present age = x | Past (n years ago) = x−n | Future (n years later) = x+n

If ratio of ages now is a:b, and n years ago was c:d:
Set up: (ax−cn)/(bx−dn) = ? and solve for x
  • Father's age = k × Son's age now. After n years, father = m × son: Solve the two equations simultaneously.
  • Average age of group changes when person joins/leaves: Change in sum = (new person's age − departing person's age). Change in average = change in sum / new count.
Classic TCS pattern
Ratio of A:B ages = 5:3. After 6 years ratio = 7:5. Find current ages.
5x+6 / 3x+6 = 7/5 → 25x+30 = 21x+42 → 4x=12 → x=3
A = 15, B = 9 years
🕐 Clocks
Minute hand: 6° per minute (360°/60)
Hour hand: 0.5° per minute (360°/720)
Relative speed = 5.5° per minute
Angle between hands at H:M = |30H − 5.5M|
Hands coincide every 65.45 min (= 720/11 min)
Hands at right angle (90°) every 32.72 min (= 360/11 min)
Angle between hands at 3:40
= |30×3 − 5.5×40| = |90−220| = 130° → 130°
📅 Calendar Problems
  • Odd days: Ordinary year = 1 odd day. Leap year = 2 odd days. Century = 5 odd days (100 yrs). 400 years = 0 odd days.
  • Day codes: 0=Sun, 1=Mon, 2=Tue, 3=Wed, 4=Thu, 5=Fri, 6=Sat
  • Leap year rule: Divisible by 4 (except centuries). Centuries must be divisible by 400. (2000 is leap, 1900 is not)
  • Day of week calculation: Add odd days to the known day. Mod 7 gives the new day.
🔢 Series & Sequences (Pattern Recognition)
  • Squares/Cubes pattern: Check if differences are consecutive odd numbers (perfect squares).
  • Fibonacci-type: Each term = sum of previous two. Check T₃ = T₁ + T₂.
  • Alternating series: Two interleaved sequences — note every 2nd term separately.
  • Geometric pattern: Divide consecutive terms to check for constant ratio.
  • n² ± constant, n³ ± constant: Find pattern by subtracting a sequence of squares/cubes.
